New Samsung S23 Ultra

Look and feel

Samsung Galaxy S23 Ultra comes in a miniature box with only one connecting cable in kit. Charger, protective case and all other accessories will have to be purchased in addition optional.

Samsung Galaxy S23 Ultra lies perfectly in the hand, this is the first sensation that is realized when familiarity with the novelty. Not all Korean smartphones were so practical: there were also too narrow edges, and heavily curved glass - and here all this is present to some extent. Smartphone not received fashionable flat surfaces and wide sidewalls, the body is streamlined in the old fashioned way. However, he was given such a shape and picked up such materials that the case does not scroll in the palm of your hand and does not strive sneak away. Even despite the maximum dimensions and large mass.

The case still has bends on the sides. The screen is protected by the latest Corning Gorilla Glass. Victus 2. Matte back in the same material. Side frame - from "reinforced" aluminum. On the sides, the frame is slightly rounded, but not as much narrowed as before. the top one and the bottom ends are completely flat. A smartphone, if desired, can be put on the table, although this is still it will not be a very stable position.

The cameras on the back are minimalist - just separate protruding lenses. Unusual creeping on the side face uniting them block-"blotch" from past seasons completely disappeared in the new series.

Screen

The Samsung Galaxy S23 Ultra is equipped with a 6.8" AMOLED (Dynamic AMOLED 2X) display inches and a resolution of 1440 × 3088, covered with Corning Gorilla Glass Victus 2. Physical dimensions screens are 73 × 157 mm, the aspect ratio is 20:9, the dot density is 500 ppi. Frame width around the screen is 2 mm from the sides, 2.5 mm from the top and 3 mm from the bottom. For screen supported 120Hz refresh rate and HDR10+.

The front surface of the screen is made in the form of a glass plate with a mirror-smooth surface, scratch resistant. Judging by the reflection of objects, the anti-glare properties of the screen are better than at the screen of Google Nexus 7 (2013) (hereinafter simply Nexus 7). Camera

    The smartphone is equipped with a main camera with a sensor as much as 200 megapixels, as well as two telephoto lenses, realizing an optical zoom, and an ultra-wide-angle module.
  • 200MP, 1/1.3″, 0.6µm, f/1.7, 24mm, multi-directional PDAF, Laser AF, OIS (primary)
  • 10MP, 1/3.52″, 1.12µm, f/2.4, 70mm, Dual Pixel PDAF, OIS, 3× optical zoom (telephoto)
  • 10MP, 1/3.52″, 1.12µm, f/4.9, 230mm, Dual Pixel PDAF, OIS, 10x optical zoom (periscope)
  • 12MP, 1/2.55″, 1.4µm, f/2.2, 13mm, 120°, Dual Pixel PDAF, Super Steady video (wide angle)

The main camera with a rare 200-megapixel Samsung sensor has three shooting modes: 50MP pixel binning (4 in 1), 12MP pixel binning (16 in 1) and full resolution 200 MP. There is a fast phase detection autofocus with a laser assistant and an optical stabilizer.

It seems that 200 megapixels is cool. But in reality, such pictures look noticeably worse than processed ones. 12 megapixels. Yes, theoretically there is some gain in detail, but it is very hard to see it. not easy: 200-megapixel picture is filled with artifacts from noise suppression, monochrome areas are “smoothed” to zero, contrasting borders are underlined. In general, looking at this is not very Nice. 50-megapixel photos have soft processing that blurs the remaining sharp edges, so the image looks soapy. And at the final stage of reduction, the processing algorithms again are passed through the picture by sharpening, so that at 12 megapixels we get a proprietary image of Samsung: sharply (sometimes - deliberately sharply) and neatly. Looks nice, but in the photos of a higher resolution makes little sense.

The smartphone can shoot video in 8K at 30 frames per second, but the picture is slightly strobe, which clearly noticeable in dynamic scenes, and besides, there is no stabilization. But when shooting at 2560 × 1440 at 60 frames per second, the image is no less clear and detailed, but much more smooth. When moving to 1080p, the picture becomes loose, but the stabilizer works perfectly. The color rendition, of course, is oversaturated, but the video sequence looks spectacular. Video Quality also very high.

Samsung Galaxy S23 Ultra is both a stylish expensive fashion solution and technically a perfect multimedia combine that can satisfy all the needs of the user at work, recreation and creativity. The only relative disadvantage against the background of modern Chinese flagships can be considered not the fastest charging, but given the huge number of advantages of this model, such a "flaw" causes happy owners, rather, a condescending smile.
